You will need access to Adobe InDesign, Illustrator, and Photoshop for this class. This can be on your personal computer or in one of the computer labs on-campus.

Personal Computer Access (BYU-Idaho Provided)

BYU-Idaho has provided you with free access to the Adobe programs because you are enrolled in this class. You can install these programs on your personal computer and you will have access to these programs until the end of the semester. See below for instructions on how to access this software on your personal computer.

Creative Cloud Subscription (All Apps)

Check out this link to see current student pricing for the Adobe Creative Cloud, which includes all the required programs for the course: https://creative.adobe.com/plans?plan=edu. This is usually billed monthly, but you are locked into a year contract.

Other Methods

The following other methods can be utilized, but problems that arise from using these will not constitute additional time for assignments or projects.

Older Downloads

You can look into older trial versions of the Adobe programs, but this is not guaranteed to work, and has limitations. Most of these trial versions give a 30 day period, but this will not span the length of the whole class. This trial period also could change at any time, leaving you without the programs. CS5 and CS6 are pretty comparable to the latest Creative Cloud versions, but there will be differences. Video tutorials, demonstrations, and other program specific instructions will also be written for Creative Cloud versions of the programs, which may require you to do a little more research if you are using the older versions.

On-campus Lab

There are several labs on campus that students can gain access to computers with the Adobe programs on them. Using these labs would require you to do all your work on-campus, which can be limiting. Open computers cannot be guaranteed, so it would be good to complete your work well before the final deadline.

Local Library

Sometimes you might find computer labs in local libraries or community centers that have the Adobe programs. This once again would have limitations, and open computers couldn’t be guaranteed.

How do I download the Adobe apps?

  1. If you already have the Creative Cloud desktop app installed skip to step number 4.
  2. Open Firefox (Chrome seems to have some issues when trying to log in.).
  3. Visit the following Creative Cloud App page and click download at the top of the page:
    https://www.adobe.com/creativecloud/desktop-app.html
  4. Open the app and run through the setup until you are prompted to sign in.
  5. Click the "Want to use your company or school account? Sign in with an Enterprise ID" link.
  6. Enter in your BYU-I email address, which will redirect you to a BYU-Idaho sign in page.
  7. Sign in with your BYU-Idaho username and password.
  8. Click on the "Apps" tab.
  9. Install, open, or Update the Adobe apps of your choice from this Creative Cloud app.

Common Questions

Where is my email?

Clutter or Junk Folder
Check your clutter or junk folder in your BYU-I email. It is common for automatically generated emails to be sorted into this folder. If you can’t find the email but still think you should have access, try the login process described on this page.

Manual Entry
We are still working on fully integrating the Adobe management system with our BYU-I systems. There might be a few oversights as we try to manually manage student access this semester. If you are enrolled in one of these classes and havn’t reveived access you should contact your instructor. Gaining access may be a little delayed, please be patient.

Can I pause my individual Adobe Cloud subscription?

No. There is no way to pause an individual Adobe subscription, but you can cancel your subscription without a cancelation fee. The University has an enterprise agreement (“ETLA”) with Adobe. Because of this benefit, individual Creative Cloud subscribers can terminate their contracts and not be charged the normal 50% fee.

Contact Adobe Support and be certain to inform the support agent that you are a student at BYU-Idaho, and you are provided Adobe Creative Cloud by the University. You may be asked to show the email informing you that you had been given access through the University.

What if I am on a waiting list?

Students on a waiting list will need to wait until they get enrolled to start the process of getting access. As soon as you are accepted into the class please reach out to your instructor and they will add you to a list that will be submitted for access.
